Practical Tips for Studying in Morocco

Complete survival guide for Moroccan & international students: visa, housing, transport, safety & daily life

Visa & Documents International Students

Everything you need to enter and stay legally in Morocco

Student Visa Process

  • Apply 2-3 months before travel at Moroccan embassy
  • Required: Acceptance letter + Passport + Photos + Bank statement
  • Visa fee: $25 - $40 depending on nationality
  • Processing time: 15-30 days

Carte de Séjour

  • Must apply within 90 days of arrival
  • Go to local police station "Sûreté Nationale"
  • Cost: 100 DH per year
  • Renew every year with university attestation

Required Documents

  • Medical certificate from home country
  • Police clearance certificate
  • 8 passport photos with white background
  • Proof of accommodation in Morocco

Housing & Accommodation

Best options for Moroccan Local & International Intl students

University Dorms Local

  • Price: 40 DH/month for Moroccans
  • Apply via university "Service des Œuvres Sociales"
  • Priority for students from far cities
  • Shared rooms 2-4 students

Private Rental Intl

  • Studio: $150-$300/month in big cities
  • Use Avito.ma, Mubawab.ma websites
  • Deposit: 2 months rent usually
  • Best areas: Agdal-Rabat, Gueliz-Marrakech

Shared Apartments

  • Cheapest option: $80-$150/month per person
  • Find roommates on Facebook groups
  • Split bills: electricity, water, internet
  • Ask for "contrat de bail" written contract

Money & Daily Life

Banking

  • Open account at Attijariwafa or BMCE Bank
  • Needed: Passport + Student card + Proof of address
  • No fees for students under 25
  • Use Wafacash for international transfers

Transport

  • Student bus card: 150 DH/semester
