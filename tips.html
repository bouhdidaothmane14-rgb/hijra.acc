Student Visa & Legal Stay

Official requirements for international students International

Long-Stay Student Visa (Type D)

Required for any study program longer than 90 days in Morocco.

  • Where to apply: Moroccan Embassy/Consulate in your country
  • Processing time: 15-30 working days
  • Visa fee: $27-$40 depending on nationality
  • Validity: 90 days, then apply for Carte de Séjour
Source: Ministry of Foreign Affairs Morocco, 2026
Required Documents

Visa Checklist

  • University acceptance letter (original)
  • Passport valid 6+ months
  • 3 passport photos (white background)
  • Bank statement: $3,000+ or sponsor letter
  • Medical certificate + Police clearance
After Arrival

Carte de Séjour

  • Deadline: Apply within 90 days of arrival
  • Where: Sûreté Nationale police station
  • Cost: 100 DH per year
  • Renewal: Yearly with university attestation
Source: DGSN Morocco, 2026

Housing & Accommodation

Options for Moroccan Local & International Intl students

Moroccan Students

University Dorms

  • Price: 40 DH/month
  • Apply: ONOUSC platform each July
  • Priority: Students from +50km away
  • Includes: Shared room, basic furniture
Source: ONOUSC, 2026
International

Private Rental

  • Studio: $150-$350/month (Rabat/Casa)
  • Platforms: Avito.ma, Mubawab.ma
  • Deposit: 2 months rent standard
  • Best areas: Agdal, Gueliz, Hay Riad
Source: Mubawab Market Report 2026

Shared Apartments

  • Cost: $80-$180/month per person
  • Find roommates: Facebook groups "Étudiants [City]"
  • Contract: Always request "contrat de bail"
  • Bills: Split water, electricity, internet

Transport & Getting Around

Public Transport 2026 Rates

  • Student bus card: 150 DH/semester (~50 DH/month) for ALSA/MDINA buses
  • Tramway Rabat/Casa: 6 DH per trip, 250 DH monthly student pass
  • Petit taxi: 7-7.50 DH daytime start, +50% from 8pm-6am. Always meter
  • CTM intercity buses: Casa-Rabat 45 DH, Casa-Marrakech 90 DH
Sources: ALSA, Casa Tramway, CTM, 2026

Safety & Scam Prevention

1. Taxi scams: Insist on meter "compteur". If refused, take another taxi.
2. Money exchange: Only use banks or official bureaux. Street rate is illegal.
3. Rentals: Never pay deposit before seeing apartment + signed contract.
Emergency: Police 19, Ambulance 15, Fire 15

Banking & Daily Life

Opening Bank Account

  • Best banks: Attijariwafa Bank, BMCE, CIH Bank
  • Required: Passport + Student card + Proof of address
  • Fees: Free for students under 25 years
  • Transfers: Use Wafacash or Remitly for intl transfers

SIM Card & Internet

  • Providers: Maroc Telecom, Orange, Inwi
  • Price: 30 DH for SIM, 10GB data = 100 DH/month
  • Required: Passport for registration
  • Home WiFi: 249 DH/month for 20MB fiber

Safety Ranking

  • Global Peace Index: Morocco ranks 74/163 worldwide
  • Safer than: USA, Brazil, South Africa
  • Rule: Avoid empty streets after midnight
  • Tip: Carry passport copy, leave original at home
Source: Global Peace Index 2025
